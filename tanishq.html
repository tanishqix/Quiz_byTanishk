Quiz of English

1. What is the correct usage of the word "enervate"?

  • To weaken
  • To invigorate
  • To inspire
  • To confuse

2. Identify the sentence with the correct subject-verb agreement:

  • None of them are coming to the party.
  • Neither of them is coming to the party.
  • Neither of them are coming to the party.
  • Neither is coming to the party.

3. Choose the correct form of the possessive pronoun:

  • It's
  • Their
  • Its
  • It

4. In which sentence is the semicolon used correctly?

  • I enjoy reading; it helps me relax.
  • I enjoy reading; it helps me relax;
  • I enjoy reading, it helps me relax.
  • I enjoy reading it helps me relax.

5. Identify the sentence with the correct use of the subjunctive mood:

  • If I was you, I would go.
  • If I were you, I would go.
  • If I am you, I would go.
  • If I were you, I will go.

6. What is the correct plural form of "octopus"?

  • Octopuses
  • Octopodes
  • Octopi
  • Octopussies

7. Choose the sentence with the correct punctuation:

  • The movie was amazing.
  • The movie, was amazing.
  • The movie was, amazing.
  • The movie was amazing!

8. Which sentence is grammatically correct?

  • Between you and I, there's a misunderstanding.
  • Between you and me, there's a misunderstanding.
  • Between me and you, there's a misunderstanding.
  • Between me and you, there's a misunderstanding.

9. Identify the sentence with the correct use of the apostrophe:

  • The cats' toys are missing.
  • The cat's toys are missing.
  • The cats toys are missing.
  • The cat toys are missing.

10. Choose the correct sentence:

  • I lie the book on the table.
  • I laid the book on the table.
  • I lay the book on the table.
  • I layed the book on the table.

11. What is the correct form of the comparative adjective for "bad"?

  • Worse
  • More bad
  • Badder
  • Baddest

12. Identify the sentence with the correct use of the hyphen:

  • It's a well known fact.
  • It's a well-known fact.
  • It's a well, known fact.
  • It's a well-known, fact.

13. Which sentence is punctuated correctly?

  • She said, "The meeting is at 2 PM".
  • She said, "The meeting is at 2 PM."
  • She said, "The meeting is at 2 PM,"
  • She said, "The meeting is at 2 PM!"

14. What is the correct plural form of "cactus"?

  • Cacti
  • Cactuses
  • Cacti
  • Cactuses and Cacti are both correct.

15. Choose the sentence with the correct use of the word "effect" vs. "affect":

  • The new law will effect the citizens emotionally.
  • The new law will affect the citizens emotionally.
  • The new law will have an affect on the citizens emotionally.
  • The new law will affect the citizens emotionally.
